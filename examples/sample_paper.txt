Title: Trust Signals in AI Advice Systems

Abstract
This study examines whether explanation quality and source disclosure affect user trust in
AI-generated financial advice. The authors conducted a controlled online experiment with
240 participants. Participants were randomly assigned to advice generated with no explanation,
a brief explanation, or a detailed explanation with source disclosure.

Introduction
Consumer adoption of AI advice systems depends on whether users understand why a
recommendation is being made. The paper asks whether transparent explanations increase trust
without increasing over-reliance.

Methods
The experiment used three conditions and measured perceived trust, intention to follow advice,
and calibration after participants reviewed six hypothetical investment scenarios. The authors
used analysis of variance and regression controls for prior investing experience.

Results
Detailed explanations with source disclosure increased reported trust by 18 percent compared
with no explanation. The effect was statistically significant at p < 0.05. Brief explanations
increased trust by 7 percent, but this result was not statistically significant. The authors found
no significant improvement in calibration, meaning participants were not better at identifying
bad advice.

Limitations
The authors state that the scenarios were hypothetical and did not involve real money. The
sample was recruited online and may not represent high-net-worth investors or professional
financial advisors. The study did not test long-term behavior or regulatory disclosure wording.

Discussion
The authors conclude that transparency can improve user trust, but trust should not be treated
as proof of better user judgment. Figure 1 shows the experimental flow. Table 2 summarizes

Conclusion
AI financial advice products may benefit from detailed explanations and source disclosure, but
additional safeguards are needed to prevent misplaced confidence.
